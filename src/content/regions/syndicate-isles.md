The Isles are colonized by Salacican aristocratic houses — nominally Salacian in religious language, secular in practice. They pay heavy taxes to the Temple on everything, legitimate and gray alike — not from devotion but because the taxes are cheaper than the alternative.

### Governing Philosophy

Commerce as the only universal sacrament. The colonial governor's loyalty runs to his house first, the Synedrion second, the Exvisar third, and the Temple when observed.

### Sacred Art

No dominant school. The Isles import and transship magical goods from every realm without developing a proprietary tradition. Cosmetic myst-application — the use of mystical energy for aesthetic enhancement — is the closest thing to a native practice. The body itself is a commercial instrument, optimized for the transaction at hand.

### Architecture & Environment

Scattered island clusters across a wide oceanic region. Colonial estates on cliffs above deep harbors. Port towns dense with throughput — warehouses, customs terminals, market wharves, and back channels that move what the front channels officially prohibit. Indigenous settlements persist in the interior hills and reef systems.

### Key Visual Details

Everything passes through. Katos fencing stolen goods from market stalls too small for humanoid vendors. Sky-Sirens nesting on cliffs. Governor's mansions transplanted from Salacican architecture onto tropical geology. The ocean is Siren territory and the islanders know it.

### Atmosphere

*A port that exists because it has to — where everything in the cosmos passes through on its way to somewhere more important and less interesting.*
