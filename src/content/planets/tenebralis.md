## The Divine Covenant

The nominal patron deity of Tenebralis is Erebus, God of Darkness. His domain encompasses all forms of darkness — physical, spiritual, and cognitive. His gift to the realm is the Shade: a dampening field that obscures Tenebralis from the divine surveillance maintained by other gods. Within the Shade, actions that would otherwise trigger divine attention or intervention remain unobserved. Erebus does not govern his people. He provides the conditions under which they are free to do as they choose without divine consequence.

In daily practice, however, the Mavarran population does not worship Erebus. They revere Vex Mavarra, their Exvisar, as the living embodiment of their cultural doctrine. The distinction between civil authority and divine authority has collapsed in Tenebralis: the Exvisar is the doctrine, the doctrine is the culture, and the culture is the closest thing the population has to a theology.

Three additional gods are entangled with Tenebralis through the Temple complex in the deep terraces of Tempora. **Ishtar, Goddess of Love, Sex, and Fertility**, whose Temple houses the Aesthetes' soul-rendering operations. **Wizaer, God of Mind and Memory**, whose Temple hosts the Second Birth ceremony. And **Ma'at, Goddess of Truth**, whose seat in the Temple complex was occupied and emptied by Vex — the pillar of absolute Truth surgically replaced with the jurisdiction of subjective, fragmented Secrets. Ishtar and Wizaer chose their alignment willingly. Ma'at was betrayed.

## The Land

Tenebralis is almost entirely uninhabitable. The surface, the upper altitudes, and the deep are claimed by organisms that evolved in permanent darkness under Erebus's domain — cognitive predators that hunt by perceiving their prey clearly, shapeless protoplasmic masses that absorb on contact, and apex entities of such cognitive scale that proximity to one constitutes a neurological injury. There is no known habitable exterior geography. The entire Mavarran population lives within a single artificially lit interior cavity: Tempora.

Tempora exists inside a hollow carved out of an interior mountain. There is no sky. The mountain closes overhead. An abyss of uncertain depth opens below. The city is built into the walls of rock, extending both laterally into the stone and vertically up and down the walls, with the abyss forming the central axis. Up is stone. Down is darkness. The inhabited space is the wall, and the wall curves away in every direction.

The native stone is onyx — black, reflective, opaque — with gold veining that catches whatever light source dominates. The architecture is subtractive: buildings are negative space left when stone is removed. Walls are polished to high gloss, producing a city where every surface is a mirror of a kind, reflecting distorted versions of whatever passes before it.

Atropa Belladonna — deadly nightshade — carpets every horizontal surface with the density and ubiquity of grass. The Mavarrans metabolize belladonna alkaloids from before birth and are functionally immune. Visitors are not.

The water is bioluminescent — cold blue-white, the city's only native light source. It enters from fissures in the upper stone and falls through the city in cascading waterfalls. The water carries trace belladonna alkaloids; sustained consumption by non-Mavarran species produces mild hallucinogenic effects, heightened suggestibility, and dissolution of short-term memory formation. Constant fog reduces ground-level visibility to approximately ten feet.

## The People

The Mavarrans are a species that evolved inside a mountain in permanent hallucinogenic darkness, surrounded by predators that hunt by perception. Their culture of deception is not a philosophical position — it is a biological survival strategy refined into civilization. The Pansilion, the masks, the fluid identity — these are the behavioral adaptations of a prey species that learned to hide from things that would devour any mind they could clearly perceive.

The primary ideology of the civilization is **freedom**. The Shim'zillai — the foundational scripture — is structured as a sequence of liberations: you may take, you may wear, you may release, you may delight, you may change, you may move unseen, you may speak, you may set down the weight, you may become someone new entirely. Read as doctrine, it is the most progressive philosophy in the cosmos. Applied without limit across centuries in a civilization with no moral floor, it produces Tempora.

The average contemporary Mavarran is not a grand schemer. Most keep their heads down, run legitimate businesses, attend the parties on the upper terraces, and avoid antagonizing anyone above their weight class. Their version of "chilled out" is still, by the standards of any other realm, a deeply predatory baseline — but most of them, most of the time, are just people navigating a system that rewards performance and punishes visibility.

The character of the civilization as perceived by outsiders is the translation layer: the sustained, disciplined performance of normalcy that Mavarrans maintain when operating abroad. Every moment of charm, every warm smile, every attentive conversation is an act of will exerted against a default baseline that would be criminal-to-monstrous by any other civilization's standards. The smile costs effort. Not guilt — effort. They are the most sober people in any room they supply narcotics to.

**Physiology:** Translucent skin with visible vasculature beneath. Non-blinking eyes, large-pupilled, calibrated for near-darkness. Retractable claws. Angular bone structure, pointed ears. Poison-immune from metabolizing belladonna alkaloids in utero. Conventionally beautiful by outside standards — the product of centuries of aesthetic culling by the Aesthetes. In practice, this form is almost never visible. The Pansilion covers it.

**The Luxenfallen** are the realm's second population. A silicon-based species with crystalline tissue underlying organic structure, inherently bioluminescent. In Tempora, they constitute the city's entire workforce, boundary patrol, and infrastructure maintenance corps. Their light illuminates the civilization that conquered them.

## Schools of Magic

**The Pansilion** — the Veilborn Mask. Universal. Every Mavarran possesses complete biological shapeshifting from dusk until sunrise. Inside Tenebralis, where no sunrise occurs, the Pansilion is permanent.

**Oneironautica** — the art of the Dreamwalker. One to five percent of the population. Entering the subconscious mind through eye contact, browsing and extracting memories, performing the Second Birth ceremony. Extracted memories are minted into Shik'piris — memory coins.

**Ak'lethem** — the Soul-Eating. The Aesthetes' practice of reaching into a living being and removing what makes them specifically that person. Rendered souls are separated into component emotional materials and refined into poisons, paralytics, narcotics, and luxury goods.

**Thanatonautica** — walking beyond death into the realms of the dead. Practiced by the Aesthetes over centuries, this discipline produced the channel through which Raiwata transited from Hel.

**The Dreamscape** — not a school but a shared metaphysical environment. All Mavarrans enter during sleep, where the Pansilion is inactive and all participants appear in their true form. A second city with its own dynamics, crimes, and power structures.

## Fashion & Material Culture

The governing principle of Mavarran fashion is **aposematic**: warning coloration. The brightest organisms in nature are the most dangerous to touch. Mavarrans are not hiding. Bright coloration functions simultaneously as warning, invitation, and demonstration that you can afford to be seen.

The operative metaphor: dress like a flower that blooms for one night. Assembled from stolen beauty. Designed to attract. Discarded at dawn. No fashion element in Tempora is native. Everything is imported, disassembled, and remixed.

The exception: operational black. When a Mavarran wears actual black, they are not hunting. They are working. Black is Isin'sartu wear. It is the most dangerous color a Mavarran can present.

Physical masks — crafted objects distinct from the Pansilion — are myst-infused material that helps anchor a specific identity for an evening. Removing a physical mask for another person is an act of intimacy more significant than most physical contact.

## Weapons & the Relationship to Force

There is no Mavarran army. There is no Mavarran military. The Pansilion reshapes bone. The body is the weapon. The real arsenal is cognitive: Oneironautica can dismantle a target's capacity for violence through a single moment of eye contact. Memory extraction removes the evidence.

A Mavarran standing unarmed in a room full of armed warriors from other realms is not the least dangerous person present. The soft power is not compensation for physical limitation. It is the preferred expression of a species entirely capable of direct violence that finds it less interesting.

If an enemy force reaches Tempora, the city's response is immediate and total: the lights go out. Half a million shapeshifters trained by annual survival culling operate in complete darkness. The city stops performing civilization and becomes what it actually is. What returns through the Clairgates is not the army that entered. It is a vector.

## Relationship to the Other Realms

**Thalassor:** Home base for operations abroad. Mavarrans are embedded at every tier of Thalassori society. The masquerade ball tradition in Thalassor is Mavarran infrastructure — seeded, popularized, and maintained. Thalassor is the distribution network.

**Ferridane:** The dismissal is the cover. Ferridanian soldiers categorize Mavarrans as dissolute layabouts and proceed to underestimate them comprehensively. The Godmind project was a Vex manipulation from inception.

**Pyrthera:** Hostile territory. Sol'Kheret's Inquisition identifies and destroys Mavarran operators with precision. The one realm where the standard operational toolkit fails reliably. Sol'Kheret refers to Vex Mavarra publicly as "Nightwhore."

**Zephyros:** The training ground. Truth is ambient. Every Mavarran tool is neutralized to some degree. Young operators treat it as a proving ground.

**Luxenfall:** The occupied territory. Formerly Luxania, the Realm of Light. Conquered approximately one thousand years ago through psychological warfare. The Lightwells — sensory deprivation facilities — remain operational. The docks in every trading port tell a different story.

## Leadership & Governance

There are no laws in Tenebralis. The Shim'zillai states that everything is permitted. In the absence of legislation, the civilization operates on three rules:

**Rule One:** Do not do anything you cannot get away with. No authority will intervene on a victim's behalf. Before crossing anyone: trace the social graph.

**Rule Two:** Do not antagonize the Aesthetes. They are the board. Everyone else is playing on it.

**Rule Three:** Do not shine through the Shade. Something cosmically loud brings attention Tenebralis cannot afford.

The **Court of Masks** handles formal disputes. Accusation requires invoking the target's Shum'katim — their hidden true name — which initiates a trial that ends in soul-death for either the accuser or the accused.

The **Isin'sartu** — the Festival of Lies — is the only calendrical event. Six days of preparation followed by a seventh day of total darkness, when Erebus reclaims all borrowed light and legal amnesty is absolute. Every survivor carries the private knowledge of what they were willing to become when the framework was absent. Every soul that falls goes to Vex.

## Societal Dysfunction

Tenebralis has eliminated every mechanism by which a civilization self-corrects. There is no law. There is no institutional accountability. There is no collective memory — the Second Birth ceremony erases every Mavarran's childhood at sixteen. There is no intergenerational continuity of experience.

The consequence is structural freefall. Each generation's behavioral baseline drifts further from recognizable norms, compounding without any mechanism to arrest the drift. The escalation is invisible from inside because no one retains the experiential memory of an earlier standard.

The population is small and shrinking. Most Mavarrans abort, because a child represents sixteen years of unmasked vulnerability in a home with no accountability. Children who survive to sixteen undergo the Second Birth, contributing their childhood suffering to the narcotics supply chain. Children whose Pansilion does not manifest do not leave the Temple.

The civilization consumes its own future and considers this rational. Its primary export is the permission to stop caring. Its primary import is souls.
