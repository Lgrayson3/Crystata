Edgerinne is the Goddess of the Blade, daughter of the elder earth god Brokkae. Her domain is refinement through sacred pressure — the principle that within all material, including the self, sleeps something luminous that can only emerge through sustained, voluntary submission to process.

She emerged from Brokkae the way a diamond emerges from stone — not by defeating the earth but by being what the earth produces when its own nature is carried to completion. For a thousand years she endured Brokkae's pressure. When she emerged, she was living diamond: skin that cuts through lies, voice that reshapes foundation, steps that leave lattice-cracks in the ground. Her sacred color is searing white. Her shrines are faceted obelisks, uncut stone at the base, flawless diamond points at the peak.

## The Covenant

Her covenant demands that the people of Ferridane submit to the same pressure. Mastery is earned, not optimized. The process is the point. This theological relationship shapes everything from how weapons are forged to how warriors are trained.

## Brokkae

The elder god Brokkae is not dead. He receded — became foundation. His symbol is the lattice: the geometry that allows light to pass through crystal cleanly. Without structure, light scatters. Without light, structure is just rock. The teaching of the founding myth is synthesis, not hierarchy.
