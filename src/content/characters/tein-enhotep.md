Tein-Enhotep is the Last Androsphinx — once called the Warbrand, sovereign of Khet five thousand years ago. He annihilated his own species with the Black Flame to free the Pyrtheran people from predation. His kingdom was the foundation of what Khet is today.

## The Black Flame

The Black Flame is the only fire in existence that truly destroys matter and energy. What it touches is gone — not transformed, not reduced, not burned to ash. Gone. Permanently removed from existence. He breathed it five thousand years ago. He has not used it in millennia. He has not met a foe that warranted it.

## The Record

His brands are not personal spiritual biography but historical record: entire wars and eras inscribed on his body. Branded down his spine, in a script only the gods can read, is the record of the First War — the foundational conflict of the cosmos, dictated to him by Nyx, Aracice, and Hestia. The gods who won that war sealed Nyx to prevent her from reading it to her son Alciel. The truth of the cosmos has been walking in the open on an Androsphinx's spine for five thousand years, untouchable and unreadable, waiting for a translator who is locked away.

## The Warbrand and the Sun Blossom

Tein-Enhotep remains alive and present in Sol'Kheret's Court. He is humorless, immovable, and carries honor for the fallen rather than grief. He trained Alciel in the discipline of the martial. He stands beside the Dragonson as godfather, bodyguard, and keeper of a promise made to his oldest friend: Aracice, the Dragon-God, who asked him to look after the boy.
