Ak'lethem *(ah-KLETH-em; Akkadian akālu "to consume" + etemmu "spirit")* — the Soul-Eating. The Aesthetes' defining practice.

## Mechanics

The power to reach into a living being and remove what makes them specifically that person while leaving the body operational. The body continues. The person does not. The resulting state is called Etemesh — a docile, vacant shell.

## Rendering

Rendered souls are separated into component emotional materials — fear, pain, joy, love — and refined into poisons, paralytics, narcotics, and luxury goods, or incorporated into Damq'eth construction. Fear rendered from children's memories during the Second Birth functions as a paralytic that freezes the body while the killing stroke is delivered.

## The Pashar'etem

The rendering ceremony, performed in the deep terraces of the Temple of Ishtar. The processing floor of a civilization whose primary import is souls.
