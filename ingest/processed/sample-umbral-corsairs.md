# The Umbral Corsairs

The Umbral Corsairs are a fleet-based faction that operates in the vast darkness between Aethermoor and Vorrenth. Unlike the planet-bound Crystal Covenant or the ideologically driven Voidborn Collective, the Corsairs are pragmatists — smugglers, salvagers, and mercenaries who exploit the power vacuums left by the two dominant factions.

## Leadership

The Corsairs are led by Captain Rhenn Ashfall, a former Voidborn operative who deserted the Collective after surviving a catastrophic void-exposure event that should have killed her. The event left her with a unique hybrid ability — she can channel both lattice resonance and void entropy simultaneously, though using both at once causes severe neurological damage.

Rhenn is a tactical genius with a reputation for impossible heists. She once stole an entire lattice node from the Shattered Reach, smuggling it aboard her flagship, the Eventide, and selling it to Voidborn researchers on Vorrenth.

## Territory

The Corsairs have no fixed territory. They operate a mobile fleet centered around the Eventide, a massive pre-Sundering vessel of unknown origin. Their primary staging area is the Drift — an uncharted region of space debris and dead satellites orbiting midway between the two known planets, used as a hidden anchorage and black market trading post.

## The Drift

The Drift is technically not a region of any planet, but a lawless zone of derelict stations and hollowed-out asteroids. It serves as neutral ground where Crystal Covenant scholars, Voidborn agents, and independent operators can trade in relative safety. Rhenn Ashfall maintains order through a simple rule: no weapons fire within the Drift's perimeter. Violators are spaced.

## Abilities

Rhenn's hybrid discipline is called Fracture Weaving — the simultaneous manipulation of lattice resonance and void entropy. She is its only known practitioner. By harmonizing constructive and destructive magical forces, she can temporarily "fracture" the boundary between structured magic and raw entropy, creating localized reality distortions that are devastatingly effective in combat but completely unpredictable.
